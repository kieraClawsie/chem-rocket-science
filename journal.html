Week 1: March 17th, 2021

This week, we decided on our topic: rocket science! Rockets, and space travel as a whole, are going to be incredibly important to the future of humanity, and chemistry is ultimately at the core of everything. By showing the chemical concepts that allow space travel to exist, we believe that we will be shedding much-needed light on the inner workings of such an important technology. Beyond that, we just think that it's going to be really interesting to learn about! We've begun looking for sources, and filtering out the less-than-reputable sources, and we believe we will be able to start developing the website by next week!

Week 2: March 23rd, 2021

Now that we were sure of our topic, we began to decide how we should tackle this project. Due to the significant amount of work ahead of us, we decided to divide and conquer. The science of rockets is incredibly varied, so choosing parts of it that correlated with the information that we learned in class was quite simple. After some preliminary research, we discussed the topics that we felt were appropriate for the project. We chose to base our presentation around the rocket fuel, electroplating, batteries, rocket boosters, and the polymers involved in the creation and operation of rockets. From here, we each chose one of the topics to become an expert in, starting our research and preparing our part to add to the website and video. This week, we also chose to divide up the task of writing out the journal entries. To keep the work divided fairly and provide varied opinions on our project, we each chose a week to journal about.

Week 3: March 31st, 2021

Our efforts in the third week were divided into 3 main sections. Firstly, we conducted extensive research on our topics to gain a deeper understanding of the chemistry behind them. This involved using numerous sources and cross checking our references. While websites and blogs were our preferred sources of information, we also used videos and research papers as well. One of the more notable events to happen during week 3 was the launch of our demonstration rockets. After countless hours of research and construction, we went to a flying field in Oakville where we successfully launched Falcon 1 and 2. After we 3D printed the rockets from PETG, it was a simple exercise to short 2 9V batteries in order to generate the spark required to ignite the motor. The combustion of the solid propellant and the oxidizer generated enough thrust to launch the model rocket. Unfortunately neither Falcon 1 or 2 were recovered. We further developed our website this week.

Week 4: April 8th, 2021

We are starting to finalize our research and believe that we have gathered enough information throughout the past three weeks on the chemistry of rocket science. Our findings in our five sections (the science behind rocket fuels/propellants, rocket boosters, the power and batteries involved, electroplating, and the polymers involved) have been very lucrative this week. We've also made a lot of progress on our website (which is almost complete) now that we have all of the necessary information needed to put onto the website. Kyle decided to code our website using HTML and CSS. We have had experience creating websites with these markup and styling languages beforehand, so the additional control given to us by writing the website ouselves has been very useful so far, and hasn't slowed us down. We feel like we are on the right track towards finish our project and we will be able to present our findings when we present on the week of the 19th!
